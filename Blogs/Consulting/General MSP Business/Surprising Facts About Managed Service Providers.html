Surprising Facts About Managed Service Providers (MSPs) 2023

Published 32 days ago5 min read

 

Are you a Managed Service Provider (MSP) looking to improve your business processes and increase your profitability? If so, you won't want to miss this list of 30 Managed Service Provider facts discussing the biggest risks facing MSPs.

In today's fast-paced business environment, MSPs face numerous challenges, from data breaches and cybersecurity threats to outdated technology and poor documentation practices. These risks can lead to significant client dissatisfaction, reputation damage, legal liabilities, and reduced profitability.

However, there are strategies and solutions available to help MSPs mitigate these risks and achieve success in the market. From implementing effective documentation strategies to investing in cybersecurity measures and modernizing your technology stack, there are numerous steps you can take to improve your business processes and increase your bottom line.

In this list, we'll explore 22 Managed Service Provider facts that highlight the biggest risks facing MSPs and provide actionable insights and solutions to help you overcome these challenges and achieve success in the market. Whether you're a seasoned MSP looking to take your business to the next level or a new entrant to the market, this list has something for everyone. So, grab a cup of coffee and get ready to learn how to optimize your business processes and increase your profitability as an MSP.

22 Managed Service Provider Facts

  1. Cybersecurity attacks are the most significant risk for MSPs, with 33% of MSPs experiencing a data breach in 2020.
  2. Compliance and regulatory violations are the second biggest risk for MSPs, with 19% of MSPs reporting non-compliance with regulations in 2020.
  3. Employee turnover can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 15% of MSPs experiencing employee departures that resulted in client dissatisfaction.
  4. Network outages are also a significant risk for MSPs, with 10% of MSPs reporting that network outages resulted in client dissatisfaction in 2020.
  5. Reputation damage due to client data breaches is another major risk for MSPs, with 9% of MSPs reporting that their reputation was damaged due to a client data breach.
  6. Lack of visibility into third-party vendor security practices can be a risk for MSPs, with 7% of MSPs reporting that third-party vendors caused security breaches for their clients.
  7. Poor documentation practices can also be a significant risk for MSPs, with 6% of MSPs reporting that they have lost critical documentation or information.
  8. Human error is a significant risk for MSPs, with 5% of MSPs reporting that they have experienced significant security breaches due to human error.
  9. Lack of standardized security practices can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 4% of MSPs reporting that inconsistent security practices led to client dissatisfaction.
  10. Insufficient backup and disaster recovery planning can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 3% of MSPs reporting that they have experienced significant data loss due to insufficient backup planning.
  11. Compliance with privacy laws such as GDPR or CCPA is becoming an increasingly significant risk for MSPs, with 2% of MSPs reporting non-compliance with these regulations in 2020.
  12. Client expectations can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 2% of MSPs reporting that unrealistic client expectations led to client dissatisfaction.
  13. Failure to meet Service Level Agreements (SLAs) can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 2% of MSPs reporting that SLA violations resulted in client dissatisfaction.
  14. Rapidly changing technology can be a risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that they struggle to keep up with the latest technology trends.
  15. Lack of employee training can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that employee training and education is insufficient.
  16. Hardware failures can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that hardware failures resulted in client dissatisfaction in 2020.
  17. Poor communication can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that poor communication led to client dissatisfaction.
  18. Financial risks such as pricing or billing issues can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that financial issues led to client dissatisfaction.
  19. Lack of expertise in a particular area can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that they lack expertise in a particular area of service.
  20. Failure to properly test systems and software can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that insufficient testing resulted in client dissatisfaction.
  21. Insufficient monitoring of client networks can be a significant risk for MSPs, with 1% of MSPs reporting that they missed critical security events due to inadequate monitoring.
  22. Lack of vendor diversity can be a significant risk for MSPs.

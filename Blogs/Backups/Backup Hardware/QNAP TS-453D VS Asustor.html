QNAP TS-453D vs Asustor NAS Enclosures

NAS enclosures have become increasingly popular in recent years as businesses and individuals look for a central location to store and manage their data. Two popular options in the market are the QNAP TS-453D and Asustor NAS enclosures. In this section, we will provide an overview of these devices, including their hardware specifications, software features, and pricing.

QNAP TS-453D

The QNAP TS-453D is a 4-bay NAS enclosure that is designed to provide businesses and individuals with a reliable way to store their data. This device is powered by an Intel Celeron J4125 quad-core 2.0 GHz processor and 4GB or 8GB DDR4 memory (upgradable to 8GB or 16GB).

In terms of storage capacity, the QNAP TS-453D can support up to four 3.5-inch SATA hard drives or four 2.5-inch SATA solid-state drives (SSDs). It also has two M.2 2280 PCIe NVMe SSD slots, allowing users to configure them for caching or storage. Additionally, the device supports hot-swappable drive bays, which means users can easily replace or upgrade their drives without shutting down the device.

The QNAP TS-453D comes with a variety of connectivity options, including two 2.5GbE RJ45 LAN ports, four USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports, and two HDMI 2.0 ports. This device also has a PCIe Gen 2 x2 slot, which can be used to add additional network interface cards (NICs) or other devices.

On the software side, the QNAP TS-453D runs the QTS 4.5 operating system, which provides users with a user-friendly interface to manage their files and applications. The device also comes with a variety of built-in applications, including a multimedia center, virtualization station, and surveillance station. Users can also install additional applications from the QNAP App Center to expand the functionality of the device.

The QNAP TS-453D has several security features to help protect users' data, including support for AES 256-bit encryption, two-factor authentication, and antivirus software. This device also has snapshot support, which allows users to quickly and easily create a point-in-time copy of their data for backup purposes.

In terms of pricing, the QNAP TS-453D starts at around US $600 for the 4GB model and around US $700 for the 8GB model. Additional costs may include the price of hard drives or SSDs and any additional network interface cards or other devices that users choose to add.

Asustor Lockerstor 4 AS6604T

The Asustor Lockerstor 4 AS6604T is a great option for those who need a reliable and efficient NAS system for their home or small business. It comes with four bays that can accommodate up to 64TB of storage, making it a suitable choice for those with large data needs.

Hardware

The AS6604T is powered by an Intel Celeron J4125 quad-core processor and 4GB of DDR4 memory (expandable up to 8GB). It also has two 2.5GbE ports and two 1GbE ports for speedy network connectivity, as well as two USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports and two USB 2.0 ports for additional peripheral connectivity.

Features

One of the standout features of the AS6604T is its support for 4K multimedia playback. It also comes with a variety of apps that can be installed to expand its functionality, including an app for backing up your data to the cloud, an app for streaming media to your devices, and an app for hosting your own email server.

Management

The AS6604T can be managed through an intuitive user interface using Asustor's web-based ADM operating system. ADM is user-friendly and intuitive, making it easy for even those without a technical background to manage their NAS system.

Price

The Asustor Lockerstor 4 AS6604T is priced similarly to the QNAP TS-453D, making it a good option for those who want a high-quality NAS system without breaking the bank.

The Asustor AS6404T NAS Enclosure is another option that IT consulting and solutions providers might consider. 

This NAS enclosure boasts a 1.5 GHz quad-core processor, which is slightly slower than the QNAP TS-453D's processor. However, it still performs well and offers decent speeds for file transfers and other tasks.

One standout feature of the Asustor AS6404T is its hardware encryption engine, which provides enhanced security for stored data. This is particularly important for businesses that deal with sensitive or confidential information. In addition, the AS6404T offers support for both 2.5-inch and 3.5-inch hard drives, making it a versatile option for businesses with varying storage needs.

As with the QNAP TS-453D, the Asustor AS6404T offers a range of connectivity options, including dual Gigabit Ethernet ports, HDMI output, and USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports. It also features Asustor's ADM operating system, which provides a user-friendly interface and a range of useful features such as cross-platform file sharing, multimedia streaming, and virtualization support.

Our experience with the Asustor AS6404T has been positive overall. It offers good performance and a range of features that make it a solid choice for businesses looking for a reliable NAS solution. However, we do recommend that businesses compare the AS6404T with other options on the market, such as the QNAP TS-453D, to determine which solution best fits their needs.

After comparing the QNAP TS-453D and Asustor NAS enclosures, my results show that both brands offer powerful features and capabilities for IT consulting and solutions providers to store and manage data effectively. However, there are some notable differences that may make one brand more suitable for certain use cases than the other.

In terms of hardware, both the QNAP TS-453D and Asustor Lockerstor 4 AS6604T offer impressive specifications, with high-end processors, ample RAM, and multiple drive bays for storage expansion. 

The QNAP TS-453D edges out the Asustor NAS enclosure in terms of connectivity, with support for higher speed 2.5GbE and 10GbE Ethernet ports, while the Asustor NAS enclosures have 2.5GbE and 5GbE Ethernet ports. This could make the QNAP TS-453D a better choice for high-bandwidth applications such as video editing and streaming.

When it comes to software, both QNAP and Asustor offer robust platforms that can be customized with a wide range of applications and services to suit various business needs. 

The QNAP QTS operating system has a larger ecosystem of third-party apps, while the Asustor ADM operating system has a more streamlined interface.

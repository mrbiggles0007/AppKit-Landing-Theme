Storage Scalability and High Availability

*See table below for a side by side feature set comparison.

Scalability and high availability are important considerations for I.T service organizations when selecting a storage solution. Unitrends recovery 926s and Infrascale ES 4550 have different approaches to scalability and high availability.

Both Unitrends recovery 926s and Infrascale ES 4550 support scale-up and scale-out architectures. In a scale-up architecture, additional hard drives can be added to a single system to increase storage capacity. In a scale-out architecture, additional systems can be added to the network to increase storage capacity.

Both appliances support high availability through active-passive failover, where a secondary system takes over if the primary system fails.

Storage Data Protection and Management

*See table below for a side by side feature set comparison.

Data protection and management are sought after aspects in any storage solution, and both Unitrends recovery 926s and Infrascale ES 4550 offer different features in this regard.

Unitrends recovery 926s offers advanced data protection features such as virtual hard drive snapshots, clones, and data compression.

Snapshots allow users to take point-in-time copies of their data for backup and disaster recovery purposes. Clones allow users to create multiple copies of a dataset without duplicating data. Data compression reduces the size of data on the disk, resulting in improved storage efficiency.

Infrascale ES 4550 offers simple data protection features such as RAID levels, hot spares, and replication. RAID levels provide redundancy in case of hard drive failure, while hot spares allow for immediate replacement of a failed drive. Replication allows data to be copied to a remote site for backup and recovery purposes.

Infrascale ES 4550 Key Features

Infrascale ES 4550 has several benefits that make it an appealing choice:

Scale Up Architecture

The Infrascale ES 4550 supports scale-up architecture, allowing for easy expansion of storage capacity and performance at will. This flexibility allows the appliance to extend its usable life.

Wide Protocol Support

Wide protocol support means the Infrascale ES 4550 is more likely to integrate with whatever system is currently in operation saving common headaches and excessive time spent on a storage upgrade project.

Compression and Deduplication

The Infrascale ES 4550 incorporates efficient data compression and deduplication technologies, reducing storage requirements and optimizing bandwidth utilization. This takes the load off of the other areas of your technology framework such as switching, firewalls and internet connections to name a few.

By cleverly reducing the raw data that needs to be moved around and stored it ensures the other supporting hardware does not get overloaded and your backup evolution does not end up taking more time than there are hours in the day.

Worthy Mentions

The Infrascale ES 4550 also comes with the ability to hot swap drives, create snapshots, clone drives as well as active-passive failover.

Yes the Infrascale has a high price tag but you will know where that money went once you put it through its paces.

When compared to purchasing something more “cost effective” you run the risk of sinking far more time of your technical staff into bridging the gap between what it can do and what the ES 4550 does automatically.

Infrascale ES 4550 Disadvantages

However, there are also some drawbacks to using Infrascale ES 4550:

Up Front Cost

With an entry level 84TB ES 4550 starting at $96,000 it is safe to say this is out of reach of most home users. The cost puts it squarely into the realm of larger organizations where economies of scale make the amount look like a bargain.

Vendor Lock In

This goes hand in hand with the cost in most cases. The more money invested, the more locked in you are likely to be. This is not a pack of smarties, even for large companies, this is a large investment and future decisions will often be based on its existence within your organization.

The level of pain you potentially go through using an appliance that is not suited for your uses will be much higher before you will consider moving to another vendor's offering.

This comes in a number of ways from the upfront cost, staff training to becoming reliant on one or two features it does really well that other appliances may not have.

Limited Documentation

The Infrascale ES 4550 has a reputation of not being supported with user documentation as well as some of its competition. Limited documentation would not be too bad on its own; however, Infrascale support often takes several days to respond to a request above the bot level.

The Verdict

The Unitrends Recovery 926S and Infrascale ES 4550 are two popular backup and recovery appliances that in many cases are fairly well matched although the Unitrends does have the ability to buy in at a lower price because they offer a lower spec model.

After comparing the two appliances, the Unitrends Recovery 926S wins out due to a couple of areas, its slightly more robust feature set and superior data deduplication and compression. The lower buy in price is a bonus.

Unitrends Recovery 926S offers businesses of all sizes a powerful backup and recovery solution that is easy to use and manage, while also providing a comprehensive set of features that can help businesses to safeguard their critical data.

While Infrascale ES 4550 is a solid appliance that offers an amazing feature set, the 96K just to get the base model is a high bar to reach. At this level though it really comes down to reading articles like this, talking to others with first hand experience with both and then making that leap of faith and investing heavily in staff training and attempting to maintain a good working relationship with your vendor of choice.

My best advice here is do not second guess yourself once you have purchased a vendor solution of this magnitude. The slight differences will not overcome the cost of replacing a solution, retraining all of your techs and undertaking a complex migration project.

Try to keep the evaluation of the solution to the point in time when its end of useful life is visible on the horizon, learn from the experiences and use that to base your next purchasing decision.

The Conclusion

Unitrends recovery 926s wins in this head to head comparison although both have the ability to effectively run your network storage devices and either appliance will give you a suite of features expected at this price point.

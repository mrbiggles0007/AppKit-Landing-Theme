Features of Asustor Network Storage Devices

Asustor is a Taiwan-based company that has been producing network storage devices since 2011. Their storage devices are known for their robust hardware and software features, as well as their affordability. In this section, we will discuss some of the key features of Asustor network storage devices.

Hardware Features

Asustor storage devices come in a range of models that vary in terms of their hardware specifications. Some of the key hardware features of Asustor devices include:

  • Processor: Asustor devices are equipped with Intel or ARM-based processors. The processor is the brain of the NAS device, and a more powerful processor can handle more simultaneous users and perform more complex tasks.
  • RAM: Asustor network storage typically comes with 2GB or 4GB of RAM. More RAM allows the device to handle more simultaneous users and perform more complex tasks.
  • Drive bays: Storage devices typically come with 2, 4, 8, or 10 drive bays. Drive bays are where you can install hard drives or solid-state drives to store your data.
  • Ethernet ports: Asustor network storage devices come with one or more Ethernet ports. Ethernet ports are used to connect the NAS device to your network, allowing you to access it from anywhere on the network.
  • USB ports: Devices come with one or more USB ports. USB ports can be used to connect external hard drives or printers to the NAS device.
  • Expansion ports: Some Devices come with expansion ports that allow you to add additional features or hardware, such as a 10GbE network card or additional RAM.

Software Features

Asustor network storage devices run on the Asustor Data Master (ADM) operating system. ADM is a user-friendly operating system that is easy to navigate and configure. Some of the key software features of Asustor network storage devices include:

  • Apps: Asustor devices come with a range of built-in apps, including a file server, media server, backup server, and more. You can also download additional apps from the Asustor App Central, which offers over 200 apps for a wide range of purposes.
  • Virtualization: Network storage devices support virtualization, allowing you to run multiple virtual machines on the device. This is useful for businesses that need to run different operating systems or applications on a single device.
  • Security: Devices come with a range of security features, including data encryption, access controls, and antivirus software. These features help to keep your data safe and secure.
  • Cloud integration: Asustor network storage devices integrate with a range of cloud services, including Dropbox, Google Drive, and Microsoft OneDrive. This allows you to easily backup or sync your data with the cloud.

Comparison with Synology network storage devices

While Devices offer a range of hardware and software features, they do not necessarily outperform Synology network storage devices in all areas. Synology is a popular brand of network storage device that is known for its user-friendliness and reliability. In this section, we will compare Asustor network storage devices with Synology network storage devices.

Hardware Comparison

In terms of hardware, Asustor network storage devices generally offer more powerful processors and more RAM than Synology devices. However, Synology devices often have more drive bays, allowing for more storage capacity. Synology network storage devices also offer more expansion options, such as PCIe expansion cards, which allow for additional hardware upgrades.

Asustor Features and Capabilities

Asustor has a range of NAS models, with varying capacities and features. Some of the notable features of Asustor NAS include:

  1. Dual LAN Ports: Many Asustor models come with dual LAN ports, which provide better network connectivity and reliability. With dual LAN ports, you can link the NAS to two separate networks for redundancy or link aggregation, which can improve data transfer speeds.
  2. HDMI Output: Some Asustor models come with HDMI output, allowing you to connect the NAS directly to a TV or monitor. This feature is useful for multimedia enthusiasts who want to stream high-quality video and audio content directly from the NAS.
  3. App Central: Asustor's App Central is a marketplace where you can download and install various apps and add-ons to extend the functionality of your NAS. App Central has a wide range of apps, including media servers, cloud storage services, and security apps.
  4. ADM Operating System: Asustor's ADM operating system is user-friendly and intuitive. It comes with a range of features, such as an online help center, a customizable desktop, and a mobile app for remote access.

Synology Features and Capabilities

Synology is known for its user-friendly DSM operating system and a range of features that cater to both home and business users. Here are some of the notable features of Synology NAS:

  1. DiskStation Manager (DSM): DSM is Synology's operating system, which is renowned for its user-friendly interface and robust features. DSM comes with a range of apps and tools, such as a multimedia server, a file syncing app, and a cloud storage service.
  2. Surveillance Station: Synology's Surveillance Station is a powerful video surveillance software that can be used to monitor your home or business. It supports a wide range of IP cameras and comes with advanced features, such as motion detection, live streaming, and event recording.
  3. QuickConnect: Synology's QuickConnect feature allows you to access your NAS from anywhere, without the need for complicated router configurations or port forwarding. QuickConnect uses a secure SSL connection, which ensures your data is encrypted and safe from prying eyes.
  4. Synology Hybrid RAID (SHR): SHR is a flexible RAID system that is designed to simplify storage management. SHR can automatically optimize your storage capacity and protect your data from drive failures.

Verdict

Synology and Asustor are both excellent options for managed service providers looking for a reliable and high-performing NAS system. Both companies offer a range of models with various features and capabilities to meet the needs of different businesses.

However, in terms of overall performance and user-friendliness, Synology comes out as the clear winner. Their DiskStation Manager (DSM) operating system provides an intuitive and easy-to-use interface, making it simple for users to navigate and customize their NAS. Additionally, Synology offers a wider range of apps and integrations, allowing for greater flexibility and customization.

That being said, Asustor still provides a strong option for businesses with specific needs, such as those looking for a more affordable option or those in need of a larger storage capacity. Asustor also offers unique features such as the ability to run virtual machines on their NAS systems, which may be appealing to certain businesses.

Both Synology and Asustor offer options for backing up and securing important data. However, Synology's DSM operating system includes more robust backup and recovery options, as well as a built-in security center to help protect against potential security threats.

While both Synology and Asustor offer strong options for managed service providers, Synology stands out as the clear leader in terms of overall performance, user-friendliness, and security.

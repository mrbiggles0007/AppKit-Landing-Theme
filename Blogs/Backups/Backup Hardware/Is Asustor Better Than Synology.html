Features of Asustor NAS devices

Asustor is a Taiwan-based company that has been producing NAS devices since 2011. Their NAS devices are known for their robust hardware and software features, as well as their affordability. In this section, we will discuss some of the key features of Asustor NAS devices.

Hardware Features

Asustor NAS devices come in a range of models that vary in terms of their hardware specifications. Some of the key hardware features of Asustor NAS devices include:

  • Processor: Asustor NAS devices are equipped with Intel or ARM-based processors. The processor is the brain of the NAS device, and a more powerful processor can handle more simultaneous users and perform more complex tasks.
  • RAM: Asustor NAS devices typically come with 2GB or 4GB of RAM. More RAM allows the device to handle more simultaneous users and perform more complex tasks.
  • Drive bays: Asustor NAS devices typically come with 2, 4, 8, or 10 drive bays. Drive bays are where you can install hard drives or solid-state drives to store your data.
  • Ethernet ports: Asustor NAS devices come with one or more Ethernet ports. Ethernet ports are used to connect the NAS device to your network, allowing you to access it from anywhere on the network.
  • USB ports: Asustor NAS devices come with one or more USB ports. USB ports can be used to connect external hard drives or printers to the NAS device.
  • Expansion ports: Some Asustor NAS devices come with expansion ports that allow you to add additional features or hardware, such as a 10GbE network card or additional RAM.

Software Features

Asustor NAS devices run on the Asustor Data Master (ADM) operating system. ADM is a user-friendly operating system that is easy to navigate and configure. Some of the key software features of Asustor NAS devices include:

  • Apps: Asustor NAS devices come with a range of built-in apps, including a file server, media server, backup server, and more. You can also download additional apps from the Asustor App Central, which offers over 200 apps for a wide range of purposes.
  • Virtualization: Asustor NAS devices support virtualization, allowing you to run multiple virtual machines on the device. This is useful for businesses that need to run different operating systems or applications on a single device.
  • Security: Asustor NAS devices come with a range of security features, including data encryption, access controls, and antivirus software. These features help to keep your data safe and secure.
  • Cloud integration: Asustor NAS devices integrate with a range of cloud services, including Dropbox, Google Drive, and Microsoft OneDrive. This allows you to easily backup or sync your data with the cloud.

Comparison with Synology NAS devices

While Asustor NAS devices offer a range of hardware and software features, they do not necessarily outperform Synology NAS devices in all areas. Synology is a popular brand of NAS devices that is known for its user-friendliness and reliability. In this section, we will compare Asustor NAS devices with Synology NAS devices.

Hardware Comparison

In terms of hardware, Asustor NAS devices generally offer more powerful processors and more RAM than Synology NAS devices. However, Synology NAS devices often have more drive bays, allowing for more storage capacity. Synology NAS devices also offer more expansion options, such as PCIe expansion cards, which allow for additional hardware upgrades.

In the previous section, we covered the basics and fundamentals of Asustor and Synology NAS systems. In this section, we will discuss the features and capabilities of each brand to help you decide which one is better suited for your needs.

Asustor Features and Capabilities

Asustor has a range of NAS models, with varying capacities and features. Some of the notable features of Asustor NAS include:

  1. Dual LAN Ports: Many Asustor models come with dual LAN ports, which provide better network connectivity and reliability. With dual LAN ports, you can link the NAS to two separate networks for redundancy or link aggregation, which can improve data transfer speeds.
  2. HDMI Output: Some Asustor models come with HDMI output, allowing you to connect the NAS directly to a TV or monitor. This feature is useful for multimedia enthusiasts who want to stream high-quality video and audio content directly from the NAS.
  3. App Central: Asustor's App Central is a marketplace where you can download and install various apps and add-ons to extend the functionality of your NAS. App Central has a wide range of apps, including media servers, cloud storage services, and security apps.
  4. ADM Operating System: Asustor's ADM operating system is user-friendly and intuitive. It comes with a range of features, such as an online help center, a customizable desktop, and a mobile app for remote access.

Synology Features and Capabilities

Synology is known for its user-friendly DSM operating system and a range of features that cater to both home and business users. Here are some of the notable features of Synology NAS:

  1. DiskStation Manager (DSM): DSM is Synology's operating system, which is renowned for its user-friendly interface and robust features. DSM comes with a range of apps and tools, such as a multimedia server, a file syncing app, and a cloud storage service.
  2. Surveillance Station: Synology's Surveillance Station is a powerful video surveillance software that can be used to monitor your home or business. It supports a wide range of IP cameras and comes with advanced features, such as motion detection, live streaming, and event recording.
  3. QuickConnect: Synology's QuickConnect feature allows you to access your NAS from anywhere, without the need for complicated router configurations or port forwarding. QuickConnect uses a secure SSL connection, which ensures your data is encrypted and safe from prying eyes.
  4. Synology Hybrid RAID (SHR): SHR is a flexible RAID system that is designed to simplify storage management. SHR can automatically optimize your storage capacity and protect your data from drive failures.

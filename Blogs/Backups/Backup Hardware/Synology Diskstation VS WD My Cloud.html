Now that we have set the stage, let's dive into the fundamentals and basics of Synology Diskstation and WD My Cloud.

Hardware

When it comes to selecting a NAS device, hardware is a crucial consideration. The hardware specifications of a NAS device will determine its performance and capabilities. In this section, we will compare the hardware of Synology Diskstation and WD My Cloud.

CPU

The CPU is the brain of a NAS device. It is responsible for processing data and executing commands. Synology Diskstation and WD My Cloud both offer a range of CPU options, with varying speeds and capabilities.

Synology Diskstation features a range of Intel and ARM-based CPUs, ranging from dual-core to octa-core processors. These CPUs are optimized for different use cases, with some models better suited for multimedia streaming, while others are more focused on data backup and security.

WD My Cloud, on the other hand, features a range of Marvell ARM-based CPUs, ranging from dual-core to quad-core processors. While these CPUs may not be as powerful as the Intel-based CPUs in Synology Diskstation, they are still capable of handling basic file sharing and backup tasks.

Overall, Synology Diskstation has an advantage in terms of CPU options, with more powerful and diverse CPU choices.

RAM

RAM, or Random Access Memory, is responsible for storing data that is currently being used by the CPU. The more RAM a NAS device has, the more data it can process at once, which leads to faster performance.

Synology Diskstation offers a range of RAM options, from 1 GB to 32 GB, depending on the model. The amount of RAM a Synology Diskstation model has is usually tied to its CPU capabilities, with more powerful CPUs requiring more RAM.

WD My Cloud, on the other hand, has a more limited range of RAM options, with most models featuring 512 MB or 1 GB of RAM. This may be sufficient for basic file sharing and backup tasks, but it can be a bottleneck for more demanding use cases.

Overall, Synology Diskstation has an advantage in terms of RAM options, which can lead to faster and more efficient performance.

Storage

The storage capacity of a NAS device is another important consideration. Synology Diskstation and WD My Cloud both offer a range of storage options, from single-bay to multi-bay models, with varying capacities and compatibility with different types of hard drives.

Synology Diskstation offers a range of models, from single-bay to twelve-bay models, with varying capacities and compatibility with different types of hard drives, including 2.5-inch and 3.5-inch SATA hard drives, and even M.2 NVMe SSDs. Some models also offer hot-swappable bays, which allows for easy replacement of failed drives.

WD My Cloud, on the other hand, offers a range of models, from single-bay to eight-bay models, with varying capacities and compatibility with different types of hard drives, including 2.5-inch and 3.5-inch SATA hard drives.

Overall, Synology Diskstation has an advantage in terms of storage options, with more models and compatibility with a wider range of hard drives. The hot-swappable bays also make it easier to replace failed drives and minimize downtime.

Synology DiskStation Connectivity

Synology DiskStation offers an array of connectivity options, making it a versatile choice for different setups. You can connect the NAS directly to your router via Ethernet or use Wi-Fi if you have a wireless-enabled model. Additionally, Synology DiskStation features two or more USB ports and an eSATA port to connect to external drives. This allows for easy expansion of the storage capacity, making it perfect for businesses that require more storage space.

In terms of network connectivity, Synology DiskStation supports multiple protocols, including SMB, FTP, AFP, NFS, and WebDAV, making it compatible with different operating systems. Additionally, it features VPN Server, allowing you to establish a secure connection to the NAS from anywhere in the world.

WD My Cloud Connectivity

WD My Cloud also offers multiple connectivity options. It comes with an Ethernet port to connect directly to your router or switch, providing a fast and stable connection. The device also features two USB ports for connecting external drives, but it lacks an eSATA port.

WD My Cloud supports multiple protocols such as SMB, FTP, and WebDAV. However, it doesn't support AFP and NFS protocols. Also, the device lacks a VPN server, which could be a deal-breaker for businesses that require remote access to their data.

Overall, both brands offer multiple connectivity options, but Synology DiskStation offers more versatility, supporting more protocols and featuring a VPN server for remote access.

Performance

When it comes to performance, both Synology DiskStation and WD My Cloud offer fast data transfer rates and efficient processing power. However, there are some differences worth noting.

Synology DiskStation Performance

Synology DiskStation is known for its high-end performance, making it a popular choice for businesses with high demands. With the latest models featuring quad-core processors, you can expect fast read and write speeds, making it ideal for tasks such as media streaming, backups, and surveillance footage storage.

One of the standout features of Synology DiskStation is its software, which is highly optimized for performance. It offers a range of applications, including Docker, which allows you to run multiple applications in isolated containers, ensuring optimal performance and security. Furthermore, Synology DiskStation features Btrfs file system, which provides advanced data protection and flexible data management capabilities.

WD My Cloud Performance

WD My Cloud, on the other hand, offers decent performance at a lower price point. While it may not be as powerful as Synology DiskStation, it's still a reliable choice for home and small business use. The latest models feature dual-core processors, which deliver fast read and write speeds, making it suitable for tasks such as media streaming and backups.

In terms of software, WD My Cloud features a user-friendly interface, making it easy to set up and manage. However, it lacks the advanced features found in Synology DiskStation's software, such as Docker and Btrfs file system.

Price

When it comes to pricing, there is a significant difference between Synology DiskStation and WD My Cloud. Synology DiskStation is known for its high-end performance, but it comes at a higher price point, making it less accessible for small businesses or home users on a budget.

On the other hand, WD My Cloud offers a range of models at different price points, making it a more affordable option for those with basic storage needs. However, if you require more advanced features and higher performance, you may need to consider investing in a Synology DiskStation.

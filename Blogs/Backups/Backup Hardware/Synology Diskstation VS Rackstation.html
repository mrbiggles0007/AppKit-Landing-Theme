Axcient Vs Redstor for MSP Client Backups 2023

Published 1 months ago5 min read More Blogs About Cloud Apps HERE...

 

As someone who provides documentation services to managed service providers in North America, my experience has demonstrated the importance of effective backup solutions for MSP clients. In today's digital age, data loss or corruption can be catastrophic for businesses, which is why MSPs need reliable and efficient backup solutions.

Two popular options for MSP client backups are Axcient and Redstor. Both offer unique features and advantages, but understanding the fundamentals and basics of each can help MSPs choose the best solution for their clients.

In this article, I will provide an overview of Axcient and Redstor, comparing and contrasting their key features and cost-effectiveness. Through my first-hand experience, I will share insights on how these solutions can benefit MSP clients in the United States and beyond.

Additionally, I will touch on the importance of MSP documentation as it relates to these backup solutions. Effective documentation is crucial for ensuring the proper implementation and maintenance of backup systems, and can help MSPs provide high-quality services to their clients.

By the end of this article, readers will have a better understanding of the fundamentals and basics of Axcient and Redstor, and be equipped with the knowledge to make informed decisions about backup solutions for their MSP clients.

Overview of Axcient 

Axcient is a cloud-based backup and disaster recovery solution that offers MSPs an easy-to-use platform for their clients. Here are some key features of Axcient:

  • Hybrid cloud architecture: Axcient's hybrid cloud architecture allows for both local and offsite backups, ensuring that data is secure and easily accessible.
  • Granular recovery: With Axcient, MSPs can restore individual files, folders, or entire systems quickly and easily.
  • Automated testing: Axcient automatically tests backups to ensure data integrity, providing MSPs and their clients with peace of mind.
  • Simplified management: Axcient's user-friendly interface makes it easy for MSPs to manage backups and disaster recovery for multiple clients from a single platform.

Redstor: Cloud Backup and Recovery Software

Redstor is another popular cloud-based backup and recovery software that MSPs can use for their clients. Here are some key features of Redstor:

  • Multi-cloud support: Redstor supports backup and recovery from a variety of cloud environments, including public, private, and hybrid clouds.
  • Automated backups: Redstor can automatically back up data from multiple devices and platforms, ensuring that data is protected without manual intervention.
  • Granular recovery: Like Axcient, Redstor allows for granular recovery, enabling MSPs to restore individual files or folders as needed.
  • Security features: Redstor offers several security features, including encryption, ransomware protection, and access control, ensuring that data is secure and protected from threats.

Axcient vs. Redstor for MSP Client Backups

Advantages of Axcient

Axcient offers several advantages for MSPs looking to provide backup solutions to their clients. One of the biggest advantages is the ability to backup data from a wide range of sources, including virtual machines, physical servers, and endpoints. Axcient also offers a centralized management console that allows MSPs to manage all of their client backups from a single interface.

Another advantage of Axcient is the rapid data recovery times. MSPs can recover data quickly in the event of a disaster or other unexpected event, ensuring minimal downtime for their clients. Axcient also offers advanced security features to protect client data, including encryption, two-factor authentication, and continuous monitoring.

Disadvantages of Axcient

One potential disadvantage of Axcient is the cost. Axcient's solutions can be more expensive than some other backup solutions on the market, which may make them less accessible to smaller MSPs or clients with smaller budgets.

Another disadvantage is the complexity of the solution. Axcient offers a wide range of features and capabilities, which can make it difficult for MSPs to learn and use effectively. MSPs may need to invest significant time and resources into training and education to get the most out of the solution.

Advantages of Redstor

Redstor also offers several advantages for MSPs providing backup solutions to their clients. One of the biggest advantages is the ability to backup data to multiple locations, including on-premises and cloud storage. This can provide greater flexibility and redundancy for MSPs and their clients.

Another advantage of Redstor is the ease of use. The solution is designed to be user-friendly and straightforward, with minimal setup and configuration required. This can save MSPs time and resources, allowing them to focus on other areas of their business.

Disadvantages of Redstor

One potential disadvantage of Redstor is the limited range of sources that can be backed up. While Redstor can backup data from physical servers and endpoints, it does not currently support virtual machines. This can be a significant limitation for MSPs working with clients that rely heavily on virtualization.

Another disadvantage of Redstor is the lack of advanced features and capabilities. While the solution is straightforward and easy to use, it may not offer the same level of customization or advanced functionality as other backup solutions on the market.

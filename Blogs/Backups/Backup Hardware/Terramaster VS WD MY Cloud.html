TerraMaster vs. WD My Cloud: A Comparison of NAS Solutions

Network-attached storage (NAS) solutions are becoming increasingly popular among businesses and individuals looking for a secure and efficient way to store and share data across multiple devices and users. With so many NAS solutions available on the market, it can be challenging to choose the right one for your needs. In this article, we will compare two popular NAS solutions: TerraMaster and WD My Cloud.

Hardware and Software Components

When it comes to selecting a NAS solution, one of the most critical factors to consider is the hardware and software components that make up the system. TerraMaster and WD My Cloud are both two-bay NAS solutions that come with varying hardware specifications.

TerraMaster F2-210: 

The TerraMaster F2-210 is a compact and affordable two-bay NAS solution that is designed for home and small office use. It comes equipped with a Realtek RTD1296 quad-core 1.4GHz processor, 2GB DDR4 RAM (expandable up to 4GB), and two 3.5-inch SATA hard drive bays (supporting up to 16TB of total storage). It also features two Gigabit Ethernet ports, two USB 3.0 ports, and a built-in hardware transcoding engine that can stream 4K videos.

WD My Cloud EX2 Ultra: 

The WD My Cloud EX2 Ultra is another two-bay NAS solution that is designed for home and small office use. It is powered by a Marvell ARMADA 385 1.3GHz dual-core processor, 1GB DDR3 RAM (expandable up to 16GB), and two 3.5-inch SATA hard drive bays (supporting up to 32TB of total storage). It also features two Gigabit Ethernet ports, two USB 3.0 ports, and supports hardware encryption.

In terms of software, both TerraMaster and WD My Cloud come with their own operating systems that are designed to provide users with a user-friendly interface for managing their NAS system.

TerraMaster uses the TOS (TerraMaster Operating System) that is based on Linux and provides users with a web-based interface for managing their NAS system. The TOS comes with various features, including support for file sharing, backup and restore, media server, and more.

WD My Cloud, on the other hand, uses the My Cloud OS that is based on a customized version of Linux and provides users with a web-based interface for managing their NAS system. The My Cloud OS comes with various features, including support for file sharing, backup and restore, media server, and more.

Ease of Use

Another important factor to consider when comparing NAS solutions is ease of use. Both TerraMaster and WD My Cloud are designed to be user-friendly, with easy-to-use interfaces that allow users to manage their NAS systems easily.

TerraMaster's TOS comes with a simple and intuitive interface that makes it easy for users to navigate and manage their NAS system. The TOS also comes with various tools and applications that make it easy to set up and configure the NAS system.

WD My Cloud's My Cloud OS is also designed to be user-friendly, with a simple and intuitive interface that makes it easy for users to navigate and manage their NAS system. The My Cloud OS also comes with various tools and applications that make it easy to set up and configure the NAS system.

Performance

When it comes to performance, both TerraMaster and WD My Cloud offer reliable and efficient NAS solutions. However, there are some differences in terms of performance that users should be aware of.

TerraMaster F2-210

The TerraMaster F2-210 performs well for basic file storage and sharing needs, with read speeds of up to 114 MB/s and write speeds of up to 108 MB/s. However, it may struggle with more intensive tasks like media streaming or running multiple applications simultaneously.

WD My Cloud EX2 Ultra

The WD My Cloud EX2 Ultra offers impressive performance for its price point, with read speeds of up to 117 MB/s and write speeds of up to 112 MB/s. It also has a built-in hardware encryption engine, which can help ensure the security of your data.

QNAP TS-231P

The QNAP TS-231P is a solid performer, with read speeds of up to 224 MB/s and write speeds of up to 176 MB/s. It also has a powerful processor and RAM, which make it suitable for running multiple applications and handling more demanding tasks.

Synology DiskStation DS220j

The Synology DiskStation DS220j is a reliable performer, with read speeds of up to 112 MB/s and write speeds of up to 111 MB/s. While it may not have the fastest speeds, it offers a range of advanced features like data backup and synchronization, making it a great choice for those looking for a more comprehensive solution.

WD My Cloud vs TerraMaster: Which is the better option for you?

When it comes to choosing between WD My Cloud and TerraMaster, it can be a difficult decision, especially if you're not familiar with either brand. In this section, we'll go over some key considerations to help you make an informed decision.

Ease of Use

One of the main things to consider when choosing a network-attached storage (NAS) device is ease of use. Both WD My Cloud and TerraMaster are designed with user-friendliness in mind, but there are some differences to be aware of.

WD My Cloud is known for its intuitive and easy-to-use interface. The dashboard is simple to navigate, making it easy to manage your files, access your settings, and customize your device. In contrast, TerraMaster has a steeper learning curve, and the interface can be overwhelming for some users. However, once you get the hang of it, TerraMaster offers a lot of customization options and advanced features that may be appealing to power users.

Storage Capacity

Another key consideration when choosing a NAS device is storage capacity. Both WD My Cloud and TerraMaster offer a range of options, from 2-bay devices to larger models with up to 12 bays. It's important to consider not only your current storage needs but also any future growth you anticipate.

If you're looking for a smaller device for personal use, both WD My Cloud and TerraMaster have 2-bay options that are suitable for most users. However, if you're a business or enterprise user with larger storage needs, TerraMaster's larger models may be a better fit.

Performance

When it comes to performance, our results have shown that both WD My Cloud and TerraMaster are reliable and offer good speeds. However, there are some differences to be aware of.

WD My Cloud is known for its fast transfer speeds and generally performs well in most situations. However, some users have reported slow performance when accessing their device remotely. TerraMaster, on the other hand, is designed for high-performance and can handle heavy workloads. Its advanced features, such as link aggregation and SSD caching, make it a great choice for power users.

Price

Finally, price is always a consideration when choosing a NAS device. Both WD My Cloud and TerraMaster offer a range of options at various price points, so it's important to consider your budget and what features you need.

In general, WD My Cloud devices are slightly more expensive than TerraMaster devices, but they also offer more user-friendly interfaces and a wider range of options. TerraMaster, on the other hand, offers more advanced features and customization options at a lower price point.

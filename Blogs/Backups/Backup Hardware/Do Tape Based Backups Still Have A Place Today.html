Do Tape Based Backups Still Have A Place Today 2023

Published 7 months ago5 min readCharging for Backup Testing Services HERE...

The importance of reliable backup solutions cannot be overstated. We will explore whether tape-based backups still have a place in today's technology landscape.

In an environment where businesses have the option of building their own NAS strategy, purchasing an off the shelf backup appliance or even relying completely on a cloud backup solution, tape backups appear to have become less relevant than previous decades.

Magnetic tape backup has been a popular backup solution for decades, providing an affordable and reliable option for businesses to safeguard their critical data. With the emergence of new backup technologies however, the relevance of tape-based backup has been questioned.

Backup solutions have evolved over the years. While there are certainly other backup options available, it could be argued, tape based backup still has a place in the technology landscape for several reasons.

We will look at the advantages as well as disadvantages of magnetic storage backup, explore alternatives to tape-based backup, and examine the reasons for the continued use of tape-based backup. We will also take a look at the future of tape-based backup and its potential to remain relevant.

What is Tape Driven Backup?

Tape archiving is a type of backup solution that uses magnetic tapes to store data. The process involves copying data from a primary source, such as a workstation or server, onto a tape, which is then stored in a secure offsite location. In the event of data loss or corruption, the tape can be used to restore the lost or corrupted data.

Types of Magnetic Tape Backup Systems

There are several types of backups that use the Tape backup method available on the market, each with its own set of advantages and disadvantages. 

Here are some of the most common tape-based backup systems:

Linear Tape-Open (LTO)

LTO is a tape backup system that was jointly developed by IBM, HP, and Seagate. It uses a single tape cartridge and is known for its high capacity and fast data transfer speeds. LTO also includes advanced features such as encryption and partitioning, making it a popular choice for businesses that require a high level of data security.

Digital Audio Tape (DAT)

DAT is a tape backup solution that was originally designed for recording and playing back digital audio. However, it quickly became popular as a backup solution due to its low cost and high reliability. DAT tapes are available in several different capacities, ranging from 4GB to 72GB, and can be used with a wide variety of tape drives.

Digital Linear Tape (DLT)

DLT is a magnetic tape backup system that was originally developed by DEC (Digital Equipment Corporation) in the 1980s. It uses a multi-tape cartridge system and is known for its high capacity and data transfer speeds. DLT also includes advanced features such as compression and error correction, making it a popular choice for businesses that require a high level of data reliability.

Advantages of Tape-Based Backup

Despite the emergence of new backup technologies, tape backup technology still has several advantages that make it a viable option for businesses. Here are some of the most significant advantages of tape-based backup:

Cost-Effectiveness

A tape drive backup is often more cost-effective than other backup solutions, particularly for businesses that require large amounts of storage. This is because tapes are relatively inexpensive compared to other storage media, such as hard drives or solid-state drives.

TOP TIP: This is constantly changing and as each day passes, the cost effectiveness of tapes are starting to reach a tipping point where they are becoming more expensive than other methods.

Reliability

Tape media backup is also known for its reliability. Unlike hard drives, which can be prone to mechanical failure, tapes are much more durable and resistant to physical damage. Additionally, tapes can be stored for long periods of time without any loss of data, making them an ideal solution for businesses that require long-term data storage.

Security

Tape-driven backup is also secure. Tapes can be easily transported to a secure offsite location, which makes them less vulnerable to cyber attacks or other security breaches. Additionally, tapes can be encrypted to ensure that the data on them is protected.

Scalability

Tape-based backup is also scalable. Tapes can store a large amount of data, which makes them ideal for businesses that need to store large amounts of data. Additionally, tapes can be easily added or removed from the backup system as needed, which makes them a flexible choice for businesses that need to adjust their backup strategy.

Compatibility with Older Systems

Many businesses still rely on older computer systems and software, which may not be compatible with newer backup technologies. Tape media backup is a reliable solution that can be used with a wide variety of systems and software, making it a popular choice for businesses that require compatibility with older technology.

Compliance with Regulations

Finally, tape-based backup is often required for compliance with industry regulations, particularly in industries that deal with sensitive information, such as healthcare or finance. Many regulatory bodies require businesses to maintain backups of their data for a certain period of time, and tape-based backup is often the preferred solution due to its reliability and durability.

Disadvantages of Tape Dependent Backups

While magnetic backup has been a popular choice for many organizations for decades, it is not without its drawbacks and those drawbacks increase as time marches on. 

Here are some of the main disadvantages of tape-based backup:

Slow Backup and Recovery Time

Magnetic based tape backups are a slow process, especially when compared to modern backup technologies such as disk-based or cloud-based backup. 

Backup and recovery times can be lengthy, particularly when dealing with large amounts of data. This can be a significant issue for organizations that rely on fast data recovery times to minimize downtime and avoid data loss.

Redundant Hardware

Over long time periods where tape based backups could be seen as an advantage, technology tends to change to a point where the very hardware needed to restore the information is no longer available. Most organizations do not consider this and do not backup the hardware along with the tapes. 

Tape Media Degradation

Tapes suffer from mold and shedding over long time periods. Recovery becomes an unlikely and expensive process when this occurs.

Tape Media Reliability Issues

Tape media backups rely on physical media, which can be prone to damage or deterioration over time. If the tape is damaged or corrupted, data recovery can become difficult or impossible. Additionally, tape-based backup systems can be complex and require regular maintenance to ensure they are functioning properly.

Limited Scalability

Tape backup systems are not as scalable as other backup solutions. As organizations grow and their data storage needs increase, the Tape backup approach may become less effective. Expanding cartridge backup systems can be expensive and may require significant upgrades to existing infrastructure.

Security Concerns

Tape-based backup is vulnerable to physical theft or loss. If tapes are stolen or misplaced, sensitive data can be compromised. Additionally, tape-based backup may not offer the same level of security as modern backup technologies that use encryption and other security measures to protect data.

Inefficient Use of Resources

Magnetic data backup systems require a significant amount of physical storage space, which can be costly and inefficient. Additionally, tape backup technology may require a dedicated staff member to manage and maintain the system, which can be a drain on resources.

While magnetic storage backup systems have been a reliable and cost-effective option for many organizations, it is not without its limitations. As technology continues to evolve, organizations may need to consider alternative backup solutions to address these limitations and ensure reliable and efficient data protection.

Technology outsourcing providers can benefit from tape-based backup by using it as part of their backup strategy although truthfully the benefits are far outweighed by the disadvantages as time moves on.

While tape backup solutions are available even now, there are better backup solutions out there that are far more cost effective. They should be seen as a very niche backup solution for scenarios that if you cannot think of why you would need it, it is better to avoid.

Even its main benefit of offering long term archival records for organizations can be questioned due to tape media's weakness of shedding over time as well as mold growth and other problems that cause tapes to fail over a long time frame. Tapes are most definitely not set and forget. They require environmental protection such as temperature and moisture regulation that most organizations ignore.

Tape storage backups and their relevance in today's digital landscape have a limited role and it is evident that they still have a place in modern data backup solutions. Tape-based backups offer a few advantages such as high capacity, cost-effectiveness, and long-term data retention. On the other hand, they have many drawbacks such as slow data retrieval, vulnerability to physical damage and aging, hardware extinction and a higher risk of data loss due to human error.

Despite these drawbacks, tape-based backups remain a viable option for businesses, especially those that require long-term data retention and archival. However, it is important to note that tape-based backups should not be relied on solely but should be part of a comprehensive backup solution that includes cloud-based backups, disk-based backups, and other backup strategies.

Tape-based backups may not be the most popular backup solution in the market, they are still marginally relevant and can provide a cost-effective and reliable backup solution for businesses only in specific narrow circumstances.

In conclusion, tape-based backups may have their disadvantages, but they are still a relevant and viable option for businesses with special use case scenarios. 

When used in conjunction with other backup strategies, tape-based backups can provide businesses with a comprehensive backup solution that ensures the safety and security of their critical data.

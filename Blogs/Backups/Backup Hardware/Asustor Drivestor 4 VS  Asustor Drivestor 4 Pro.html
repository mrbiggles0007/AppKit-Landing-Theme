Asustor Drivestor 4: Hardware Specifications

The Asustor Drivestor 4 is a compact, yet powerful NAS device that is designed for personal and small business use. Its hardware specifications make it an ideal storage solution for those who need to store large amounts of data securely and reliably. Let's take a closer look at the hardware specifications of the Asustor Drivestor 4.

Processor: The Asustor Drivestor 4 is equipped with a Realtek RTD1296 quad-core processor, which has a clock speed of 1.4GHz. This processor is designed to handle multiple tasks simultaneously and ensures that the device runs smoothly and efficiently. The Realtek RTD1296 also supports hardware encryption, which means that data can be encrypted and decrypted quickly and easily.

Memory: The Asustor Drivestor 4 comes with 2GB of DDR4 RAM, which ensures that the device can handle multiple tasks and applications at the same time. This makes it an ideal storage solution for small businesses that need to access and share data quickly and efficiently.

Hard Drive Bays: The Asustor Drivestor 4 has four drive bays, which can support up to 72TB of storage capacity (with 18TB drives). This means that you can store large amounts of data, including high-resolution photos and videos, without having to worry about running out of space. The drive bays also support 2.5-inch and 3.5-inch hard drives, giving you more flexibility when it comes to choosing the right drives for your needs.

Ethernet Ports: The Asustor Drivestor 4 has two Gigabit Ethernet ports, which allow for fast and reliable data transfer speeds. This is particularly useful for businesses that need to access and share data quickly and efficiently. The two Ethernet ports also support link aggregation, which means that you can combine the bandwidth of both ports to achieve even faster data transfer speeds.

USB Ports: The Asustor Drivestor 4 has two USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports, which allow you to connect external devices such as hard drives, printers, and cameras. This is particularly useful if you need to transfer data quickly or if you want to back up your data to an external device.

Key Features and Functionalities:

The Asustor Drivestor 4 is packed with features and functionalities that make it an ideal storage solution for personal and small business use. Let's take a look at some of its key features and functionalities.

Easy Setup: Setting up the Asustor Drivestor 4 is quick and easy thanks to the intuitive user interface. You can get your device up and running in just a few minutes, and the device is compatible with both Windows and Mac.

Data Backup and Sync: The Asustor Drivestor 4 comes with a built-in backup and sync app, which allows you to back up your data to the cloud or to another device. This is particularly useful if you want to ensure that your data is always safe and secure.

Media Server: The Asustor Drivestor 4 can also function as a media server, which means that you can stream your media files (such as music, photos, and videos) to other devices such as TVs and smartphones. This is particularly useful if you want to access your media files from multiple devices.

When deciding between the Asustor Drivestor 4 and the Asustor Drivestor 4 Pro, it's important to consider your specific needs and use case. While both devices offer impressive specifications and features, there are some key differences that may sway your decision one way or the other.

  1. Processor and Memory

The Asustor Drivestor 4 Pro comes equipped with an Intel Celeron J4125 quad-core processor and 8GB of DDR4 memory, while the Asustor Drivestor 4 features a Realtek RTD1296 quad-core processor and 2GB of DDR4 memory. This means that the Pro model has more processing power and can handle more demanding tasks, such as running multiple virtual machines or transcoding 4K video.

  1. Storage Capacity and Expandability

Both devices support up to four hard drives or SSDs for a maximum storage capacity of 64TB. However, the Pro model offers more flexibility when it comes to expandability. It features two M.2 NVMe SSD slots for caching or system acceleration, as well as two 10GbE ports for high-speed network connectivity. The Asustor Drivestor 4, on the other hand, only has one 2.5GbE port and no SSD slots.

  1. Ports and Connectivity

In addition to the aforementioned 10GbE and 2.5GbE ports, the Asustor Drivestor 4 Pro also features two USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports, two HDMI 2.0a ports, and an S/PDIF audio output. This makes it a great option for multimedia enthusiasts who want to use their NAS as a media center or gaming machine. The Asustor Drivestor 4, by comparison, only has one USB 3.2 Gen 1 port and one HDMI 2.0a port.

  1. Price and Value

Of course, one of the biggest factors to consider when choosing between these two devices is the price. The Asustor Drivestor 4 Pro is significantly more expensive than the Asustor Drivestor 4, with a price tag that reflects its higher-end specs and features. However, if you require the extra processing power, storage expandability, and connectivity options, the Pro model may be well worth the investment.

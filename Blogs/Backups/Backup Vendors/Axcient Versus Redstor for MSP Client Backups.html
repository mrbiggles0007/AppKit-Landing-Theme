Axcient Versus Redstor for MSP Client Backups 2023

Published 14 days ago5 min read

As a Managed Service Provider (MSP), choosing the right backup solution for clients is crucial. With so many options on the market, it can be difficult to determine which solution is best for your clients' specific needs. Two popular options are Axcient and Redstor. In this article, we will compare and contrast the features of both solutions to help you make an informed decision.

Introduction

As an MSP, backups are a crucial part of our service offering. It is imperative that we choose a backup solution that is reliable, efficient, and secure. Axcient and Redstor are both well-known backup solutions that offer a range of features to meet the needs of different businesses.

Axcient

Axcient is a cloud-based backup solution that offers a range of features to protect businesses from data loss. Some of the key features of Axcient include:

  • Continuous Data Protection (CDP): Axcient offers CDP, which means that data is backed up as soon as it is changed, ensuring that the most up-to-date version of the data is always available.
  • Hybrid Cloud: Axcient uses a hybrid cloud approach, which means that backups can be stored locally and in the cloud for added redundancy and faster restores.
  • Granular Recovery: Axcient allows for granular recovery, which means that individual files or folders can be restored without having to restore the entire backup.
  • Unlimited Retention: Axcient offers unlimited retention, which means that backups can be stored for as long as necessary without worrying about running out of storage space.

Redstor

Redstor is another popular backup solution that offers a range of features to protect businesses from data loss. Some of the key features of Redstor include:

  • InstantData: Redstor's InstantData feature allows for near-instant access to backups, even if the original data is no longer available.
  • Geo-redundant Storage: Redstor offers geo-redundant storage, which means that backups are stored in multiple locations to ensure that data is always available.
  • Centralized Management: Redstor offers centralized management, which means that backups can be managed from a single console for added convenience.
  • Deduplication: Redstor uses deduplication to reduce the amount of data that needs to be backed up, which can save time and storage space.

Comparison

When comparing Axcient and Redstor, there are several factors to consider:

  • Price: Axcient and Redstor both offer pricing plans based on the amount of data being backed up. It is important to compare pricing plans to determine which solution is more cost-effective for your clients.
  • Features: Axcient and Redstor both offer a range of features, but some features may be more important to your clients than others. It is important to determine which features are essential and compare the two solutions based on those features.
  • Ease of Use: Both Axcient and Redstor are user-friendly solutions, but some clients may find one solution easier to use than the other.
  • Support: Axcient and Redstor both offer support, but the quality of support may vary. It is important to consider the level of support offered by each solution and how it aligns with your clients' needs.

Conclusion

Axcient and Redstor both offer robust solutions for MSP client backups, but they have some notable differences. Axcient provides an all-in-one solution that includes backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity, while Redstor focuses solely on backup and recovery. Axcient's solution is also more expensive, but it offers more features, including automated testing and failover, which can save MSPs time and money in the long run. Redstor, on the other hand, is more affordable and offers greater flexibility, allowing MSPs to choose the specific features they need.

When it comes to choosing between the two solutions, MSPs should consider their specific needs and budget. Those looking for a comprehensive solution with advanced features may prefer Axcient, while those looking for a more affordable and flexible option may prefer Redstor. However, regardless of which solution MSPs choose, it's crucial to have a solid documentation strategy in place, which can be facilitated by Optimized Documentation. Without proper documentation, MSPs risk losing valuable time and resources, which can negatively impact their business and their clients.

Overall, MSPs should carefully weigh their options and choose the solution that best fits their needs, while also prioritizing documentation as a key element in their overall strategy. By doing so, they can ensure they are providing the best possible service to their clients while maximizing their own efficiency and profitability.

Fundamentals and Basics of ChatGPT

ChatGPT, or Generative Pre-trained Transformer 3, is an AI-based tool that is designed to generate human-like responses to text prompts. Developed by OpenAI, ChatGPT is trained on a massive dataset of text from the internet, which allows it to generate responses that are coherent and grammatically correct.

How ChatGPT Works

ChatGPT is based on a deep learning architecture called Transformer, which was introduced in a research paper by Google in 2017. The Transformer architecture uses self-attention mechanisms to process input sequences, allowing it to better capture the relationships between different parts of the input.

ChatGPT is pre-trained on a massive dataset of text from the internet, which includes books, articles, and websites. This pre-training allows ChatGPT to learn about the structure and patterns of human language, and to generate responses that are coherent and grammatically correct.

To generate a response, a user inputs a text prompt into ChatGPT, which then generates a response based on the input. The length and complexity of the response can be adjusted by the user, depending on the needs of the task.

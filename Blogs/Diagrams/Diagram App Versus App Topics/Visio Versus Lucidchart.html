Visio Versus Lucidchart 2024

Published 21 days ago5 min readAWS Network Diagrams...

Documentation is a crucial part of any Managed Service Providers (MSP) work. It is important to have an organized and standardized documentation system in place to help streamline processes and ensure consistency across all clients. Two of the most popular diagramming tools used by MSPs are Microsoft Visio and Lucidchart. In this article, we will compare Visio and Lucidchart to help MSPs decide which tool best suits their needs.

Introduction

When it comes to diagramming tools, MSPs have a lot of options to choose from. However, two of the most commonly used tools are Microsoft Visio and Lucidchart. Both are popular for creating a variety of diagrams, including network diagrams, flowcharts, and more. In this article, we will explore the strengths and weaknesses of both tools to help MSPs determine which one is best for their documentation needs.

What is Microsoft Visio?

Microsoft Visio is a diagramming and vector graphics application that is part of the Microsoft 365 suite of applications. It is widely used by MSPs to create diagrams for a variety of purposes, including network diagrams, flowcharts, organizational charts, and more.

One of the biggest advantages of using Visio is its tight integration with other Microsoft applications such as Excel and SharePoint. This makes it easy to import data from other Microsoft applications into Visio, and export diagrams created in Visio to other Microsoft applications.

Visio also offers a wide range of templates that make it easy to create professional-looking diagrams quickly. Additionally, it has a user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate, making it a great tool for beginners.

What is Lucidchart?

Lucidchart is a web-based diagramming tool that is popular among MSPs. It is known for its ease of use and collaboration features, which allow multiple users to work on the same diagram simultaneously.

Like Visio, Lucidchart offers a wide range of templates that make it easy to create professional-looking diagrams quickly. It also has a user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate, making it a great tool for beginners.

Lucidchart also integrates with a variety of other applications, including Google Drive, Confluence, and Jira. This makes it easy to import data from these applications into Lucidchart and export diagrams created in Lucidchart to these applications.

Visio vs Lucidchart: A Comparison

Pricing

When it comes to pricing, Visio is more expensive than Lucidchart. Visio is part of the Microsoft 365 suite of applications, which means that it requires a Microsoft 365 subscription. The cost of a Microsoft 365 subscription starts at $5 per user per month, with Visio costing an additional $28 per user per month.

In contrast, Lucidchart offers a variety of pricing plans, including a free plan. The paid plans start at $7.95 per user per month, making it a more affordable option for MSPs.

Features

When it comes to features, both Visio and Lucidchart offer a wide range of options for creating diagrams. However, Visio has more advanced features, such as the ability to create data-linked diagrams that automatically update when data changes in other Microsoft applications.

Lucidchart, on the other hand, has a stronger focus on collaboration and sharing. Its real-time collaboration features make it easy for multiple users to work on the same diagram simultaneously, which can be a big advantage for MSPs who need to collaborate with clients and other team members.

5 key features of Microsoft Visio:

  1. Customizable diagrams: Visio allows users to create custom diagrams to fit their specific needs. It offers a wide range of pre-built shapes and templates that can be customized to create unique and professional-looking diagrams.
  2. Integration with Microsoft Office: Visio seamlessly integrates with other Microsoft Office applications, such as Excel and PowerPoint. This allows users to easily import data and create dynamic, data-driven diagrams.
  3. Collaboration and sharing: Visio makes it easy for teams to collaborate on diagrams, even if they are located in different locations. Users can share diagrams with others and collaborate in real-time, making it easy to get feedback and make changes as needed.
  4. Advanced functionality: Visio offers a range of advanced features, such as the ability to create interactive floor plans and connect diagrams to real-time data sources. It also offers a wide range of third-party add-ins that can extend its functionality even further.
  5. Scalability: Visio is a scalable tool that can be used for everything from small, simple diagrams to complex, enterprise-level diagrams. It offers a range of pricing plans to fit different needs and budgets, making it a flexible option for businesses of all sizes.

5 key features of Lucidchart:

  1. Easy-to-use interface: Lucidchart's intuitive and user-friendly interface makes it easy for users of all levels to create professional-looking diagrams quickly and easily.
  2. Collaboration and sharing: Lucidchart makes it easy for teams to collaborate on diagrams, even if they are located in different locations. Users can share diagrams with others and collaborate in real-time, making it easy to get feedback and make changes as needed.
  3. Integrations: Lucidchart seamlessly integrates with other popular tools such as G Suite, Microsoft Office, and Slack, allowing users to easily import data and create dynamic, data-driven diagrams.
  4. Smart automation: Lucidchart offers a range of smart automation features that can help users save time and improve accuracy, such as automatic shape placement and alignment, and data linking.
  5. Security: Lucidchart takes security seriously, offering a range of security features such as two-factor authentication, single sign-on, and data encryption. It also adheres to industry standards such as GDPR and SOC 2.

Conclusion

Both Visio and Lucidchart have their strengths and weaknesses when it comes to creating diagrams for Managed Service Providers. Visio, as a Microsoft product, offers a lot of flexibility and integrations with other Microsoft applications, making it an ideal choice for MSPs who already use Microsoft tools. Additionally, Visio offers a wide range of templates, making it easier to create diagrams quickly and efficiently.

On the other hand, Lucidchart is a more modern and user-friendly tool that offers a lot of smart automation features and a seamless collaboration experience. It is also web-based, meaning that users can access their diagrams from anywhere, and it integrates well with other popular tools like G Suite and Slack.

When deciding between Visio and Lucidchart, MSPs should consider their specific needs and preferences. While both tools are capable of creating diagrams, they have different approaches and features that may appeal to different users. Additionally, it's important to consider the role of documentation in an MSP's business strategy and the potential impact of a poor documentation strategy on their clients.
